











Welcome to
Database Architecture.

My name is Charles Severance,
and I am a faculty member
at the University of Michigan
School of Information.

In this course,
we are going to step back
from programming syntax
and frameworks
and look at something deeper.

How modern data systems
actually work.

Most programmers learn SQL.

Some programmers learn NoSQL.

But very few programmers
understand why these systems
exist in the first place.

Why Google built new kinds
of databases.

How Facebook changed
database scaling forever.

Why cloud computing forced
new architectural tradeoffs.

And why modern systems
blend ideas from both
traditional relational databases
and distributed NoSQL systems.

This course is about
those ideas.

We will talk about:

ACID and BASE architectures.

Replication and sharding.

Consistency and scalability.

We will look at how systems evolved
from centralized SQL servers
to globally distributed cloud systems.

And we will study
the engineering decisions
that shaped modern Internet
applications.

This is not a vendor course.

This is not a certification course.

This is an architecture course.

The goal is to help you become
a master programmer.

A programmer who understands
not just code,
but the systems,
tradeoffs,
and historical forces
behind modern software.

Along the way,
we will explore systems
like PostgreSQL,
MongoDB,
FoundationDB,
Deno KV,
and cloud-scale architectures
inspired by companies
like Google,
Amazon,
and Facebook.

We will connect history,
engineering,
and practical design.

And by the end of the course,
you will have a much clearer picture
of why modern systems
are designed the way they are.

So welcome to the course.

I am glad you are here.

Let’s get started.
